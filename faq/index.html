Frequently Asked Questions

  • What does Waltz do with my passwords?

    The first time you log in to any site with Waltz, you will be asked for your username and password. Waltz stores an encrypted version of your credentials in your browser's local storage.

    Your credentials are encrypted using AES. The ciphers are 2048-bit random keys generated by Cy.

  • Does Waltz change my passwords?

    Definitely not. Waltz stores encrypted versions of the credentials you give it, and logs in using those. Waltz will never change your password without you explicity asking.

    You can view all of the credentials that Waltz has stored by going to the options page.

  • Can I use Waltz on mobile?

    Waltz does not work on mobile yet. You can still log in to mobile sites using your username and password like you normally would.

    Sites that have integrated with Clef will allow you to log in on mobile without a password.

  • Can I have multiple accounts for one site?

    Unfortunately, right now Waltz only supports one account per site. Multiple account support is very high on our roadmap, though. Keep an eye on our blog and our our twitter to hear when new features are added.

  • Why doesn't Waltz work on every site?

    In order for Waltz to enabled features like login from anywhere and instant logout, every site needs to be manually configured. We're working to add configurations as quickly as we can. You can request sites for us to add by creating a Github Issue.
